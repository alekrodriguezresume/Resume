Project Management Fun Facts

The Great Pyramids were one of the earliest large-scale project management efforts—without software or email!
Source: PMI.org
Gantt charts are over 100 years old, introduced by Henry Gantt in the 1910s.
Source: Gantt.com
Only around 35% of projects are considered truly successful in meeting all goals.
Source: PMI Pulse of the Profession
Scope creep is a leading cause of project failure—it sneaks up when goals keep changing.
Source: PMI.org
The word “project” comes from the Latin “projectum,” meaning to throw forth.
Source: Etymology Dictionary
The Agile Manifesto was created at a ski resort in Utah in 2001.
Source: AgileManifesto.org
The PMP® certification is considered the gold standard for project managers worldwide.
Source: PMI.org
PERT charts were first developed by the U.S. Navy in the 1950s to manage missile projects.
Source: Defense Acquisition University
“Death march projects” describe doomed efforts with unrealistic goals but no way out.
Source: Edward Yourdon – *Death March*
Project managers spend up to 90% of their time communicating, not planning.
Source: PMBOK & PMI.org
Project management is used across every industry—from tech to tacos.
Source: PMI Industry Insights
The World Bank called project management a core global workforce skill.
Source: World Bank Blogs
